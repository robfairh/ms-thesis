\subsection{Phase I Exercise 1}

The benchmark provides the cross-sections required to conduct the exercise in the separate file \textit{OECD-MHTGR350\_Simplified.xs}.
This ensures a common data-set among various benchmark participants and allows to compare stand-alone neutronic predictions with no thermal-fluids feedback.
A separate file \textit{xsmap.pdf} specifies a detailed map of the cross-section numbering for the different  layers of the core.
The core comprises 232 hexagonal subdomains for which the benchmark provides their cross sections.
We differentiate 6 different macroscopic regions listed in Table \ref{tab:mac-region}.

\begin{table}[htbp!]
  \centering
  \caption{.}
  \label{tab:mac-region}
  \begin{tabular}{@{}l c}
  \toprule
  Macroscopic region    & Subdomains     \\
  \midrule
  Fuel              & 1 to 220      \\
  Bottom reflector  & 221 to 224    \\
  Inner reflector   & 225           \\
  Outer reflector   & 226-227       \\
  Top reflector     & 228 to 231    \\
  Control Rod       & 232           \\
  \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The multi-group macroscopic cross-sections were obtained using DRAGON-4 \cite{marleau_user_2016} from a full block configuration with a double heterogeneity treatment of the fuel.
The cross-sections are homogenized over the block and exhibit a 26 energy group structure.

\subsection{Cross section handling}

% % Info provided by the benchmark
% Normalized flux, \Sigma_t, D, \nu\Sigma_f, \Sigma_f, \chi_T, P0 Scattering Matrix \Sigma_{s0, g \rightarrow g'}, P1 Scattering Matrix \Sigma_{s1, g \rightarrow g'}, \Kappa = 200 MeV

% % Info required by Moltres
% \Sigma_r, \nu\Sigma_f, D, \beta_{eff}, \Sigma_f, \Kappa, v^{-1}, \chi_T, \chi_D, P0 Scattering Matrix \Sigma_{s0, g \rightarrow g'}, \lambda

The cross-section format that Moltres uses is slightly different to the format of the benchmark cross sections.
For such reason, we had to write a script to handle the formatting differences.
The most important difference is the treatment of the removal reactions.
While Moltres uses the removal cross-section in its calculations, the benchmark provides the total and the scattering cross-sections.
Using equation \ref{eq:removal} \cite{massimo_basic_1976} we carried out the calculation of the removal cross section.

\begin{align}
    \Sigma_g^r= \Sigma_g^t-\Sigma_{g \rightarrow g}^s \label{eq:removal}
  \intertext{where}
  g &= \mbox{group $g$} \notag \\
  \Sigma_g^r &= \mbox{group $g$ removal cross section} \notag \\
  \Sigma_{g \rightarrow g}^s &= \mbox{group $g$ to group $g$ scattering cross-section}
\end{align}

We also conducted a parametric study where we collapse the cross-sections to a smaller number of energy groups.
In such study we analyze the impact over the accuracy, computational time, and memory requirements of Moltres simulations.
We carried out the group collapsing by means of equation \ref{eq:collapse1} to \ref{eq:collapse4} and equation \ref{eq:removal} \cite{duderstadt_nuclear_1975}.

% Duderstadt 7-54
\begin{align}
  \phi_{G} &= \sum_g \phi_g \label{eq:collapse1} \\
  \Sigma_x^G &= \frac{\sum_g \Sigma_x^g \phi_g}{\sum_g \phi_g} \label{eq:collapse2} \\
  \Sigma_s^{G \rightarrow G'} &= \frac{\sum_g \sum_{g'} \Sigma_s^{g \rightarrow g'} \phi_g}{\sum_g \phi_g} \label{eq:collapse3} \\
  \chi_t^G &= \sum_g \chi_t^g \label{eq:collapse4}
  \intertext{where}
  \phi_{G} &= \mbox{group G neutron flux} \notag \\
  \Sigma_x &= \{\Sigma_t, \nu\Sigma_f\} \notag
  \Sigma_{g \rightarrow g}^s &= \mbox{group $g$ to group $g$ scattering cross-section}
\end{align}
